Without Consequence, Without Regret

21 February 2019

In a debut album riddled with purposeful responses to those who claimed she’d never make it in the industry, Alice Merton‘s strong-willed and persevering lyrics are impaired by what may be assumed as her insecurities. As the bass hits, those impactful moments are narrowly missed or avoided. It’s almost as if Merton’s search for a “home” can be felt even in the denial that it is underway. Merton can be heard fighting to live on her own terms and timing while borrowing from others. Continue reading...

Cab For Cutie - Kintsugi

16 June 2015

With so many new albums released every year, how are we, the listener, supposed to relate to each and every one? It’s just not possible. At least not at the very moment of its release. When I heard Michael Franti & Spearhead’s Yell Fire! in 2006, I couldn’t relate. Or, I wasn’t in the mood to hear it and relate to it. Music takes time.
