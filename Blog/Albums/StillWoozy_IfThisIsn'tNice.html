Still Woozy - If This Isn't Nice, I Don't Know What Is

29 August 2021

Matthew Pate

Having had the change to see Still Woozy at a music festival and sample some of his tracks in 2019, I was moderately intrigued and looking forward to his debut album.

Setting the stage with Woof, Sven Eric Gamsky (Still Woozy), treats the listener with a tempo and beat reminiscent of Ra Ra Riot and Hot Chip while, offering a peak into his mind as he sings,

There you go, your ducks in a row / So many things you'll never know / If you want it I can show you but....You wouldn't last a day in my head....You'd probably go insane / You's probably never be the same again.

Gamsky, while inviting the listener to spend a day in his mind, then denies entry, I bet you wanna know / Why I keep my windows closed / It's not baggage it's cargo... and claims that his experiences, failures, depressions were somehow more difficult, worse, or deeper than others', You like to think that you had it rough / But I'm gonna call your bluff / And you pray it's been enough....

While the track is narrow-minded and egocentric, it remains to be the best and most enjoyable of the album.

It is not until track 5 that we're rewarded with Get By. The up beat, foot-tappable rhythm inaccurately coaxes the listener into giving up another 23 minutes of valuable listening time.

Lyrically, the album reminds me of something I would have written as a depressed teenager with a limited vocabulary and a disdain for the thesaurus. There is about as much originality and lyrical variation as The Beatles' Love Me Do. In fact, this incredible lack of creativity and uniqueness is readily apparent in Get Down. Gamsky's blatant affinity for Daft Punk's Get Lucky is glaringly obvious in both, title and lyricism.

At moments I am transported to a coffeeshop with Hot Chip's The Warning whispering from the loudspeakers. But the moments pass quickly, as they often do in If This Isn't Nice, I Don't Know What Is. The hole from which Gamsky is seen attempting to crawl out of on the album's cover is assuredly one of self-absorption, dug on his own. There, I said it. You - Gamsky - dug your own grave.

I'll take a softly played Hot Chip or Glass Animals album over Still Woozy's If This Isn't Nice, I Don't Know What Is any day.
