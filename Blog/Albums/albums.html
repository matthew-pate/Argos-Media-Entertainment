Alice Merton
Mint

21 February 2019 - Matthew Pate

In a debut album riddled with purposeful responses to those who claimed she’d never make it in the industry, Alice Merton‘s strong-willed and persevering lyrics are impaired by what may be assumed as her insecurities. As the bass hits, those impactful moments are narrowly missed or avoided. It’s almost as if Merton’s search for a home can be felt even in the denial that it is underway. Merton can be heard fighting to live on her own terms and timing while borrowing from others. more...

Nada Surf
The Stars are Indifferent to Astronomy

13 February 2012 - Matthew Pate

Nada Surf are Indifferent to the Mainstream

About a week or two before the album dropped on January 24, 2012, NPR began streaming Nada Surf‘s new album, The Stars Are Indifferent to Astronomy, in its entirety. I listened to the album about six times straight through, two times a day, on my way to and from work.


Without fail, every Nada Surf release reminds me of adolescence and how, regardless of the stress and disappointment, there’s always something to be optimistic about. This reminiscence isn’t due to the lyrics, rather it is due to the sloppy arrangement of instruments which the lyrics combine with. Nada Surf reminds me of a teenage garage band breaking in to the scene. more...
